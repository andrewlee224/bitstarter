The process works as follows:

  1. Sign up and declare your goal
  2. Based on your personal data, statistics, and cooperation model, our web application assigns you a partner
  3. Report your own progress, and assess your partner's progress. Share advice on pursuing your goal effectively

There are two possible cooperation models, each suited for different activities:

  1. Location-dependent - your partner is chosen by location, among other factors. This way you can organize meetups and possibly physically collaborate on your goal
  2. Location-independent - your partner is chosen independently of their location. All the feedback and assessment is done via our web application

FAQ

Does this really work?
Short answer - yes.
Long answer - there is a well-documented psychological phenomenon called Kohler Effect. In short, it means that people want to achieve the level of competence of those they interact with.
I'm interested. Any interesting studies to read up?
There has been a study done by B. Irwin of Michigan State's University Department of Kinesiology in which the performance of exercising participants was measured. It turned out that when participants exercised while watching a live broadcast of their exercising partner, their performance levels increased greatly. So it's worth noting that exercising partners located at fairly large distances can still reap benefits of shared motivation.
